The Four Pieces

Four installs. Sit on top of the existing brand.

No course rebuild. No re-platform. No brand pivot. The four pieces patch the gaps between the audience, the buy decision, and the warm buyer base.

Piece 01 · The Paid Mid-Tier
01

The AC Service Tech Operator Track - two parallel quarterly cohorts above the book line.

The audience is built and free. 500K-plus YouTube subs, an active podcast, three books, and an Instagram following. The books are the only paid product line and they price one-time at book-tier. There is no paid mid-tier between the free portal and the book line. The fix is the Operator Track. Two parallel cohorts: the Apprentice-to-Master Cohort (10 weeks, 1-to-5-yr tech climbing the credential ladder) and the Shop Owner Cohort (8 weeks, 1-to-5-tech shop owner scaffolding the business side). Caps at 25-30 per cycle. Quarterly cadence. Mid-four-figure pricing.

We build the cohort landing copy, the application Typeform, the offer architecture document, the welcome-and-onboarding email sequence, the cohort Skool or Circle setup, and the application-review playbook. Craig runs the cohort sessions.

Year-1 floor across both cohort tracks: 540K to 990K incremental on the cohort line alone.

Piece 02 · The Pre-Sell Asset
02

The 5-7 minute VSL on acservicetech.com + the book product pages + the Operator Track landing.

The buy decision currently happens on a static Wix surface with no operator-grounded video pre-sell, and on Amazon listings with no embed slot. The 500K-plus YouTube audience that watches Craig at the gauge for 15-30 min per video lands on a text-and-image page at the moment of purchase. The fix is a single record day that produces three cuts: a 5-7 min homepage VSL on acservicetech.com hero, a 3-5 min book-tier VSL on the acservicetech.com book product surfaces, and a 5-7 min cohort-tier VSL on the Operator Track landing hero.

We script, direct the record, edit, caption, and embed the three cuts. The voice on screen is Craig's. The B-roll is the gauge, the manifold, the meter, the system. No lifestyle frames.

Conservative pre-sell lift on the cohort line alone: 80K to 180K Year-1 incremental.

Piece 03 · The Upstream Router
03

The "Where Is Your HVAC Career In The Operator Ladder?" readiness scorecard.

The 500K-plus YouTube + podcast + book + Instagram audience exits the funnel into either an Amazon book purchase or a free YouTube subscribe with no email opt-in above the podcast subscribe. There is no lead magnet at scale. The fix is a 90-second 12-question scorecard returning a four-band result (Too Early / Ready For Apprentice-to-Master / Ready For Shop Owner / Too Far Along). Captures email, tags by years-on-tools, state, current revenue band, cohort-track preference. The scorecard is the upstream router for the cohort, the book line, and the email list.

We install the scorecard. Question logic, scoring algorithm, routing rules, 3-line bottleneck readouts per band, email tagging. Craig approves the question set and the band thresholds.

15K to 40K incremental Year-1 subscribers. 120K to 280K incremental Year-1 cohort revenue from the scorecard-sourced waitlist.

Piece 04 · The Segmented Welcome Flow
04

The four-branch flow underneath the book buyer, the podcast subscriber, the scorecard completer, and the cohort applicant.

The four buyer types convert on different signals and ladder up the value chain at different speeds. A book buyer who reads cover to cover is the highest cohort-conversion prospect. A podcast subscriber with 30-plus episodes consumed is the second. A scorecard completer is the third (forked per band). A cohort applicant who did not make this cycle is the next-cycle priority. Today, all four route into the same downstream because no segmented welcome flow exists. The fix is the four-branch flow with cross-branch suppression rules.

We build the four welcome sequences, the trigger logic in the email platform, the segmentation tags, the cross-branch routing rules, and the re-engagement layer for not-approved cohort applicants. Craig approves the copy register on each branch.

40K to 110K Year-1 incremental across cohort fill, repeat book purchase lift, and podcast-to-list conversion.

A Note On Frame

Operator-development cohort, every surface.

The HVAC technical education space sits next to state licensure, EPA 608, and manufacturer-certified training. Every public surface in this package stays at the level of operator-development scaffolding. The cohort does not promise a license, does not replace an apprenticeship, does not substitute for EPA 608, and does not substitute for manufacturer training. The official providers stay official. The cohort scaffolds operator discipline AROUND the credential ladder. The technical curriculum is Craig's. AG1 does not pretend to teach refrigerant charging or system diagnosis. AG1 builds the funnel infrastructure underneath the brand.
