The 5-7 minute homepage VSL section-by-section. Operator-grounded register. Craig on camera at the workbench or in the van. No lifestyle B-roll. No motivational music. The voice on screen is Craig's.

Two supporting VSL scripts (3-5 min book-tier VSL for the acservicetech.com book product surfaces, 5-7 min cohort-tier VSL for the Operator Track landing) sit below the homepage script. All three cuts come out of one record day.

VSL 1: The Homepage VSL (5-7 minutes)

Embed location: acservicetech.com hero Goal: Route the visitor to the scorecard or the cohort waitlist Camera: Craig at the workbench. Tools visible (gauge, manifold, meter, system). No lifestyle frames in the cut. Wardrobe: Working register (collared shirt or workshirt, not a suit, not lifestyle apparel) Music: None, or a very quiet operator-register bed at low gain. No motivational track.

Section 1: The Open (0:00 - 0:35)

Hey - Craig Migliaccio. I run AC Service Tech.

If you have hit this page, you probably found me on YouTube, on the podcast, or off one of the books. The free content covers the technical procedure end of the trade. Refrigerant charging, mini split service, system diagnosis. The books ride along in the van.

The cohort I am opening today is the layer above the book line. Two parallel tracks for two different operators. I will walk you through what they are, who they are for, and how to find out which one is the right fit in 90 seconds.

Section 2: The Two Cohort Tracks (0:35 - 2:00)

The first cohort is the Apprentice-to-Master Cohort. 10 weeks. For the working tech with 1 to 5 years on tools who wants the scaffolding to climb the credential ladder faster. State license prep adjacency. Technical content review. Operator-discipline scaffolding.

The cohort does not replace your apprenticeship program, your state license, your EPA 608, or your manufacturer training. The official providers stay official. The cohort sits around the credential ladder, not on top of it.

The second cohort is the Shop Owner Cohort. 8 weeks. For the 1-to-5-tech shop owner or the senior tech ready to start a shop. The cohort covers the business of HVAC underneath the technical work. Pricing structure, dispatch rhythm, technician training cadence, recurring maintenance contract structure.

The cohort does not promise a specific revenue number. The cohort scaffolds the operator who is already running a shop or starting one.

Section 3: Why These Cohorts Exist (2:00 - 3:30)

The two cohorts exist because the questions I get every week on the podcast are the same two questions, asked in two different shapes.

Shape one is the working tech asking how to climb the credential ladder faster. The free YouTube tutorials cover the technical content. The books cover the procedure. What is not in the free content is the operator-discipline scaffolding behind the climb. The Apprentice-to-Master Cohort is that scaffolding.

Shape two is the shop owner asking how to scaffold the business side without sacrificing the technical work. The free podcast covers the technical questions. The books cover the technical procedure. What is not in the free content is the operator-discipline scaffolding behind running a shop. The Shop Owner Cohort is that scaffolding.

Neither cohort teaches HVAC. The cohorts teach operator discipline. The HVAC technical content is already in the books, the YouTube channel, and the podcast.

Section 4: The Cohort Format (3:30 - 4:45)

Both cohorts run on the same format spine.

Capped per cycle. Application-gated. Quarterly cadence. Mid-four-figure pricing. Manual review on the application.

Weekly live call cadence with me. Office-hours blocks twice a month. Private cohort channel for the duration. Journal templates and scaffolding worksheets handed off on cycle start. Graduation criteria is plan adherence over a 60-day window, not a revenue claim.

The cap holds. Quarterly cadence means the next cycle is always near. The application closes when the cap is full.

Section 5: The Scorecard (4:45 - 5:45)

The fastest way to know which cohort track is the right fit is the scorecard linked below this video.

12 questions. 90 seconds. Four band result options.

Band 1 is too early. Routes back to the free YouTube playlist and the entry book.

Band 2 is ready for the Apprentice-to-Master Cohort.

Band 3 is ready for the Shop Owner Cohort.

Band 4 is past the cohort tracks. Routes to the podcast and an opt-in to discuss advisory work, if that is the right fit.

You get a 3-line readout on where you sit and what the next step is. The readout is useful even if the cohort is not the right fit yet.

Section 6: The Close (5:45 - 6:30)

The scorecard link is the button below this video. The full cohort detail and the application surface is the second link.

If you have been on the channel a while, on the podcast a while, or have one of the books on the van shelf, this is the layer above the book line. The same voice, the same register, more depth.

Operator-development cohort. Not ticketed trade training. EPA 608, state licensure, and manufacturer-certified programs stay with the official providers. Results vary by market, capital, and operator execution.
