AC Service Tech LLC | Copy Strategy

Voice rules, register separation, hero claims locked across the package. This is the calibration layer that keeps every public-facing surface aligned with Craig's technician-grounded register.

The Voice Rules (Operator-To-Operator About The Funnel)

What the voice IS

  • Technician-to-technician, plain English
  • Operator-grounded direct (the gauge, the manifold, the meter, the system)
  • Time-on-tools-respected (the audience is licensed techs, apprentices, and students - they know more about HVAC than AG1 ever will)
  • Low jargon ceiling above the technical terms (where the technical term is the right word, use it; otherwise plain English)
  • Respectful of the credential ladder (state license, EPA 608, manufacturer-certified, apprentice program all referenced as official)
  • One-on-one register (the surface speaks to one tech, one shop owner, one applicant at a time)

What the voice IS NOT

  • Not motivational-guru register
  • Not "make $X with me" framing
  • Not lifestyle B-roll register
  • Not "easy money in the trades" register
  • Not "leave your job, escape the rat race" register
  • Not corporate-agency register (no "synergy", "stakeholder", "ecosystem", "bandwidth")
  • Not soft-coach register (no "transform your life", "unlock your potential", "next level")

Why this matters

Craig's audience is the working HVAC tech. They have a low tolerance for marketing-speak. The voice that converts the YouTube subscriber is the voice that must convert the cohort applicant. The pitch surface is the cohort itself, not the tech's job.

Register Separation (AG1-Built Surfaces vs. Craig's Existing Surfaces)

Craig's existing surfaces (unchanged)

  • YouTube tutorials, podcast episodes, IG clips, book content - all stay in Craig's voice and on Craig's curriculum
  • AG1 does not write or rewrite a single line of HVAC technical content

AG1-built surfaces (new)

  • Cohort landing page (landing-page.html)
  • Pitch hub (hub-page.html)
  • VSL scripts (vsl-preview)

  • Outreach copy (outreach)

These surfaces speak about the FUNNEL underneath AC Service Tech LLC. They do not speak about HVAC technical content. AG1's voice on these surfaces is operator-to-operator about the funnel, not technician-to-technician about the trade.

The split

Craig writes (or has written) the technical curriculum. AG1 writes the funnel surfaces. The two registers do not blend. The cohort sells operator development. The books sell technical procedure. The two product lines complement, they do not compete.

Niche Vocabulary Bans (The Words That Break The Brand)

The technician-education brand breaks if any of these registers leak onto the AG1-built surfaces. This list is for the AG1 write team, not for Craig.

Motivational / guru register

  • Any "manifest", "abundance", "limitless", "unstoppable", "destiny"
  • Any "next level", "level up", "unlock", "transform", "elevate"
  • Any "secret", "hack", "blueprint to riches", "passive income while you sleep"
  • Any "freedom from the trade", "escape the trade", "leave your job"

Lifestyle / income-claim register

  • Any specific dollar amount the cohort participant will make
  • Any "make $X per month / per year / per call"
  • Any rev-per-tech promise
  • Any "fire your boss" framing
  • Any "build your empire" framing

Soft-coach register

  • "Your journey", "your transformation", "your dream"
  • "Step into your power", "step into your purpose"
  • Any version of "you got this"

Corporate-agency register

  • "Synergy", "stakeholder", "ecosystem", "bandwidth", "leverage" (as a verb), "circle back", "touch base"
  • "Game-changer", "revolutionary", "disruptive"
  • "Cutting-edge", "state-of-the-art", "next-generation"

Em-dash specifically

The em-dash character is banned across every deliverable except the literal sign-off pattern "- Andrei" at the close of a DM or email. Use a hyphen for ranges, a colon for lists, or a sentence break for emphasis.

Tone Calibration For The Cohort Tier

The cohort tier is mid-four-figure pricing. The register at this price band is:

  • Sober (not hype)
  • Specific (the cohort is the Apprentice-to-Master or the Shop Owner track, not "the next-level program")
  • Capped (25-30 seats per cycle, quarterly cadence, not "open enrolment forever")
  • Application-gated (not "click here to buy now")
  • Operator-development framed (not "make-money-in-HVAC framed")

The cohort tier conversion comes from the audience trust Craig has built over years. The AG1 copy job is to NOT break that trust at the application surface.
