AC Service Tech LLC | Bottleneck Analysis

Lead doc for the package. Each bottleneck below is one observable gap, the AG1 service-mapped fix, the install scope, and a Year-1 floor math range. The four bottlenecks compound. Numbers ladder, not replace.

Bottleneck 1: No productised paid mid-tier between the free YouTube/podcast/IG audience and the books

What is observable

The audience layer is built and free. 500K-plus YouTube subscribers, an active podcast ("AC Service Tech Answers", Apple Podcasts ID 1522359430), Instagram @acservicetech short-form, and three Amazon books (book one alone reports 60,000-plus copies sold). The books are the only paid product line. They price one-time at book-tier pricing. There is no paid mid-tier between the free portal and the books, and there is no paid tier above the books at all.

Why this is the headline gap

The book-buyer count alone (60K-plus on book one, multiply across the three-book line) is the warmest possible audience for a paid mid-tier and currently has no upgrade path. The 500K-plus YouTube audience is even broader. The audience is built. The audience is buying. The product line caps at book-tier pricing per transaction. Every peer in the technician-education category eventually patches this with a productised paid cohort, a paid library subscription, or both. The line is open.

The AG1 service-mapped fix

We install the AC Service Tech Operator Track as two parallel cohorts that respect the brand's technician-grounded register:

  • Apprentice-to-Master Cohort - 10-week structured program for the working tech with 1 to 5 years on tools who wants the scaffolding to climb the credential ladder faster (state license prep adjacency, not replacement). Operator-development scaffolding around the official apprentice and license programs.
  • HVAC Shop Owner Cohort - 8-week program for the 1-to-5-tech shop owner or the senior tech ready to start a shop. Business-of-HVAC operator scaffolding (pricing, dispatch rhythm, technician training cadence, recurring-maintenance contract structure). Not a sales course, not a marketing course, not a "leave the trade" course. Shop-owner discipline scaffolding.

Caps per cycle: 25 to 30. Cadence: quarterly. Pricing: mid-four-figure. Cohort feeds the books, the podcast, and the YouTube channel for cross-promotion (cohort buyers become higher-AOV viewers and reviewers across the existing surfaces).

Install scope (what AG1 builds, what Craig does)

AG1 builds the cohort landing copy, the application Typeform (operator-readiness scoring), the cohort offer architecture document, the welcome-and-onboarding email sequence, the cohort Skool or Circle community setup, and the application-review playbook. Craig runs the cohort sessions (the curriculum is his) and approves the final scaffolding.

Year-1 floor math (operator-development cohort line only)

Apprentice-to-Master Cohort: 4 cycles per year, 25 to 30 seats per cycle, mid-four-figure pricing. Year-1 floor on this line alone: 300K to 540K.

Shop Owner Cohort: 4 cycles per year, 20 to 25 seats per cycle, mid-four-figure pricing. Year-1 floor on this line alone: 240K to 450K.

Stacked Year-1 floor across both tracks: 540K to 990K incremental on top of existing book line, existing YouTube AdSense, existing podcast monetisation, and existing acservicetech.com merch.

Conservative assumption set: 75 percent of cohorts run, 80 percent capacity fill rate, no upsell to a second cycle, no add-on training revenue, no in-person component. The numbers carry pure cohort-tier revenue only.

The frame

Operator-development cohort. Not ticketed trade training. EPA 608, state licensure, and manufacturer-certified programs stay with the existing official providers. The cohort scaffolds the operator who is already on tools or already running a shop.

Bottleneck 2: No VSL on acservicetech.com, no VSL on the book sales surfaces, no VSL on the Operator Track landing when it launches

What is observable

The buy decision currently happens on:

  • A static acservicetech.com home/product surface (Wix-hosted, image and text, no operator-grounded video pre-sell above the buy)
  • Amazon book listing pages (Amazon-native layout, no embedded VSL slot)
  • The future Operator Track landing (does not exist yet, so the VSL slot is open from day one)

Craig has a 500K-plus YouTube audience that watches him at the gauge, the manifold, and the meter for 15 to 30 minutes per video. The book-buyer audience already knows the voice from YouTube. The static buy surfaces are not using the asset that converts the audience in the first place.

Why this matters

The pre-sell asset that builds the audience is video. The buy decision is happening on a text-and-image page. The pre-sell signal that converted the YouTube subscriber and the podcast listener is missing at the moment of purchase. The conversion lift from a 5-7 minute homepage VSL on a brand whose audience converted on video in the first place is one of the most consistent moves in the playbook.

The AG1 service-mapped fix

We script, direct the record, edit, and embed a 5-7 minute homepage VSL on acservicetech.com hero, a 3-5 minute book-tier VSL on the book sales surfaces (where the platform allows embed, otherwise a linked watch page on acservicetech.com), and a 5-7 minute cohort-tier VSL on the Operator Track landing hero. The VSLs are filmed in Craig's existing operator-grounded register. No lifestyle B-roll. No motivational music. The voice on screen is Craig's.

Install scope

AG1 writes the three VSL scripts (homepage, book-tier, cohort-tier), directs the record day (remote or in-person), edits the cuts, captions, and embeds. Craig records once and the three cuts come out of one shoot day.

Year-1 floor math

Conservative pre-sell lift across the three surfaces, attributed to incremental cohort sales (the book line moves slowly so the book-tier VSL is treated as a long-tail brand lift, not a Year-1 line item). Year-1 floor on the cohort line from VSL lift alone: 80K to 180K.

The frame

The VSL is operator-grounded. The voice on screen is Craig's. The B-roll is the gauge, the manifold, the meter, the system. No lifestyle frames in the cut. No "make $X" frames in the cut.

Bottleneck 3: No readiness scorecard, no lead magnet above the podcast subscribe, no segmented email list

What is observable

The audience exits the funnel into either an Amazon book purchase, a YouTube subscribe, an Instagram follow, or a podcast subscribe. There is no observable email opt-in above the podcast subscribe (no scorecard, no free tip list, no quick-reference download). The email asset (which the books should be feeding) is not visible on the public surfaces.

Why this matters

The 500K-plus YouTube audience and the 60K-plus book-one buyers represent the warmest possible email list in the technician-education category. Without an email opt-in surface above the free assets, the list does not exist at scale. Without the list, the cohort launches have no in-house demand, the book line has no nurture, and the podcast and YouTube subscribers stay platform-locked.

The AG1 service-mapped fix

We install the "Where Is Your HVAC Career In The Operator Ladder?" scorecard. A 90-second 12-question readiness quiz that returns a four-band result:

  • Band 1: Too early (apprentice, less than 1 year on tools) - routed to the free YouTube playlist and the entry book
  • Band 2: Ready for Apprentice-to-Master Cohort (1 to 5 years on tools, no shop) - routed to the cohort waitlist
  • Band 3: Ready for Shop Owner Cohort (5-plus years, running or starting a shop) - routed to the cohort waitlist
  • Band 4: Too far along (multi-shop owner, contractor scale) - routed to the podcast/community and an opt-in to be contacted for advisory work

The scorecard captures email, tags by years-on-tools, state, current revenue band (for shop-owner respondents), and cohort-track preference. The scorecard is the upstream router for the cohort, the book line, and the email list. It also surfaces a 3-line bottleneck readout per respondent so the quiz itself is useful even if the respondent never buys.

Install scope

AG1 builds the scorecard (question logic, scoring algorithm, routing rules, bottleneck readouts, email tagging), the routing landing pages for each band, the scorecard nurture sequence (forked per band), and the email-tagging infrastructure. Craig approves the question set and the band thresholds.

Year-1 floor math

15K to 40K incremental Year-1 subscribers off the scorecard alone. Conversion to cohort waitlist at 6 to 10 percent of bands 2 and 3 respondents. Year-1 incremental cohort revenue from the scorecard list alone: 120K to 280K (folded into the cohort line above, not double-counted).

The frame

The scorecard is operator-development calibration, not aptitude testing. The Too Early band routes back to free assets respectfully. The Too Far Along band routes to advisory, not sales pressure.

Bottleneck 4: No segmented welcome flow underneath the book buyer, the podcast subscriber, the YouTube long-tail viewer, or the cohort applicant

What is observable

The Amazon book buyer routes into the standard Amazon post-purchase flow (out of Craig's control). The podcast subscriber sits on the Apple/Spotify subscribe list with no email touch. The YouTube long-tail viewer is platform-locked with no email touch. The (future) cohort applicant has no segmented welcome flow because the cohort does not exist yet.

Why this matters

The four buyer types convert on different signals and ladder up the value chain at different speeds. A book buyer who reads cover to cover is the highest cohort-conversion prospect. A podcast subscriber who consumes 30-plus episodes is a second-highest cohort-conversion prospect. A YouTube long-tail viewer who watched 5-plus tutorial uploads is the third. A cohort applicant who did not get approved this cycle is the highest re-application target for the next cycle. All four route into the same downstream today because there is no segmented welcome flow.

The AG1 service-mapped fix

We install a four-branch segmented welcome flow:

  • Branch 1 (Book Buyer Email-Captured): Triggered when a book buyer opts into the bonus chapter download. Welcome sequence frames the book as the foundation, the cohort as the scaffolding above it.
  • Branch 2 (Podcast Subscriber Email-Captured): Triggered when a podcast subscriber opts into the show-note download or the scorecard. Welcome sequence frames the podcast as the cadence, the cohort as the deep dive.
  • Branch 3 (Scorecard Completer, Forked Per Band): Triggered on scorecard completion. Welcome sequence forks per band per Bottleneck 3 above.
  • Branch 4 (Cohort Applicant - Approved And Not-Approved): Triggered on application submit. Approved applicants route to enrolment. Not-approved applicants route to the next-cycle waitlist and a 6-week nurture preparing them for re-application.

Install scope

AG1 builds the four welcome sequences, the trigger logic in the email platform (ConvertKit or similar), the segmentation tags, the cross-branch routing rules, and the re-engagement layer. Craig approves the copy register on each branch.

Year-1 floor math

40K to 110K Year-1 incremental from the segmented flow alone (cohort fill lift, repeat book purchase lift, podcast-to-list conversion lift). Folded into the cohort line above for the cohort lift portion, separate for the book and podcast portion.

The frame

The four branches do not change the existing free assets. The free YouTube stays free. The free podcast stays free. The free Instagram stays free. The welcome flow sits underneath the email capture, not on top of the audience surfaces.

Stacked Year-1 Floor (Conservative)

Across the four installs, treating cohort revenue as the headline line and the supporting bottlenecks as multipliers rather than separate revenue lines:

  • Cohort line (Bottleneck 1): 540K to 990K
  • VSL pre-sell lift (Bottleneck 2): 80K to 180K (incremental on the cohort line)
  • Scorecard lift (Bottleneck 3): folded into the cohort line via the scorecard-sourced waitlist
  • Segmented welcome flow lift (Bottleneck 4): 40K to 110K (incremental across cohort, book, and podcast lines)

Stacked Year-1 floor: 660K to 1.28M incremental on top of existing book line, existing YouTube AdSense, existing podcast monetisation, and existing Wix merch.

The numbers carry the conservative assumption set (75 percent cohort run rate, 80 percent fill rate, no in-person upsell, no advisory-tier revenue from the Too Far Along band, no Amazon catalogue lift attribution).

What This Bottleneck Map Is Not

This map does NOT touch:

  • HVAC technical curriculum (Craig owns the curriculum, AG1 does not pretend to)
  • State HVAC licensure (the cohort sits around licensure scaffolding, not in place of it)
  • EPA 608 certification (stays with the official testing centres)
  • Manufacturer-certified programs (stay with the manufacturers)
  • Trade school accreditation (the cohort is operator-development, not accredited training)

The compliance footer applies to every surface: "Operator-development cohort. Not ticketed trade training. EPA 608, state licensure, and manufacturer-certified programs stay with the existing official providers. Results vary by market, capital, and operator execution."
