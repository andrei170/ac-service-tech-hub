Email 1.2 (Day 3): The why behind the cohort

Subject: Why the Operator Track exists

Hi,

Quick read.

The books I write cover the technical procedure end of the trade. Refrigerant charging, mini split service, quick-reference cards. Foundation-level depth, designed to ride along in the van.

The questions I get every week on the podcast are the next layer up. How do I climb the credential ladder faster. How do I prepare for my master license exam. How do I scaffold the business side of the shop without sacrificing the technical work. Those questions do not fit in a book.

The Operator Track is the cohort I built for those questions. Two parallel cohorts: the Apprentice-to-Master Cohort (1-to-5 years on tools, climbing the credential ladder), and the Shop Owner Cohort (1-to-5-tech shop owner, scaffolding the business side).

Capped per cycle. Application-gated. Quarterly cadence. Operator-development frame. The cohort does not replace your apprenticeship, your state license, your EPA 608, or your manufacturer training. The official providers stay official. The cohort sits around the credential ladder.

If the layer above the book is the part you have been asking about: [Operator Track URL]

Craig

Sequence Architecture (Cross-Branch Suppression)

Cross-branch rules to avoid sequence collision:

  • A scorecard completer who is already on Branch 1 (Book Buyer) pauses Branch 1 and enters Branch 3 per band
  • A cohort applicant pauses every other branch and enters Branch 4
  • A not-approved cohort applicant re-enters Branch 3 per band on the next cycle invitation
  • A Band 4 (Too Far Along) respondent pauses Branch 1 / Branch 2 and stays on the advisory opt-in cadence

The cross-branch suppression rules live in the ConvertKit (or equivalent) automation layer. The four branch sequences and the cross-suppression layer is the full scope of the email install.
